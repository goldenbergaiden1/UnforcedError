Cut — Quote doesn't exist

Each violation of the final order may result in a civil penalty of up to $51,744.

We looked for this sentence and it is not in the document:
Each violation of such an order may result in a civil penalty of up to $51,744
We searched the document for this exact sentence. It isn't there. It turned out to be from the FTC's press release, not the order we cited.

Something we learned, and don't love

Two separate AI systems check every fact. One sees only the quote and the claim, nothing else. The other gets the whole document and is told to prove the claim wrong.

We expected the second one — the aggressive one — to be the strict one. It wasn't. It approved "Illuminate paid a ransom" even though the document only says "agreed to pay." The one that saw less caught it.

Our guess: giving the checker the full document gave it enough context to talk itself into agreeing. They disagreed on 1 of 26 facts. We're publishing that number because a checking system whose flaws you can't see isn't a checking system.
